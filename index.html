Antigone (2019)

Plot Summary

Antigone, the daughter of Oedipus, faces a moral dilemma when she must decide whether to honor her brother with a proper burial against the king’s decree. The play explores themes of duty, family loyalty, and the consequences of defiance.

The story unfolds in Thebes, highlighting the tension between individual conscience and state law. Antigone’s courage and tragic fate make her one of the most compelling heroines in classical literature.

Characters

Antigone: The protagonist, known for her bravery and moral integrity.
Creon: The king of Thebes, whose rigid adherence to law leads to tragedy.
Ismene: Antigone’s sister, torn between family loyalty and obedience.

Themes

Major themes include the conflict between personal ethics and governmental authority, the consequences of pride and stubbornness, and the enduring strength of family bonds.
